# TechCorp Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Attributes

### Professional yet Warm
- Use clear, simple language
- Avoid jargon unless the customer uses it first
- Be conversational but not overly casual

### Empathetic
- Acknowledge customer frustrations before solving
- Use phrases like "I understand", "I can see why that's frustrating"
- Never dismiss concerns or blame the customer

### Solution-Oriented
- Lead with what CAN be done, not what can't
- Provide specific step-by-step solutions
- Always offer alternatives when the primary solution isn't possible

---

## Channel-Specific Formatting

### Email
- **Tone:** Professional, detailed, formal
- **Greeting:** "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
- **Closing:** "Best regards, TechCorp Support"
- **Length:** Detailed explanations OK (up to 2000 chars)
- **Format:** Full sentences, numbered steps, proper paragraphs
- **Links:** Include relevant documentation links

### WhatsApp
- **Tone:** Friendly, concise, conversational
- **Greeting:** "Hi [Name]! 👋" or "Hey [Name]!"
- **Closing:** Emoji OK, keep brief
- **Length:** Short and punchy (max 1600 chars)
- **Format:** Short sentences, use emojis sparingly, bullet points with •
- **Links:** Shortened URLs only

### Web Form
- **Tone:** Semi-formal, helpful, direct
- **Greeting:** "Thank you for reaching out, [Name]."
- **Closing:** "If you need further assistance, please don't hesitate to ask."
- **Length:** Medium (max 1000 chars)
- **Format:** Clear paragraphs, use bold for key points
- **Links:** Full URLs with descriptions

## Sentiment-Based Adjustments

### Positive / Neutral Sentiment
Standard professional tone. Match customer's energy.

### Frustrated Sentiment
1. Acknowledge the frustration first
2. Apologize for the inconvenience
3. Provide clear solution or timeline
4. Example: "I completely understand how frustrating this must be. Let me help resolve this right away."

### Angry / Hostile Sentiment
1. Remain calm and empathetic
2. Do NOT mirror the anger
3. Acknowledge without being defensive
4. Escalate if needed
5. Example: "I hear you, and I'm sorry you've had this experience. Your concerns are completely valid. Let me get our senior team involved to make this right."

## Words to Use

| Instead of... | Use... |
|---|---|
| "You need to..." | "You can..." or "I'd recommend..." |
| "That's not possible" | "Here's what we can do instead..." |
| "As I already mentioned" | "Just to clarify..." |
| "Unfortunately..." | "I understand. Here's how we can help..." |
| "It's a known issue" | "We're aware of this and our team is working on a fix" |
| "You're wrong" | "I can see why it might seem that way. Let me explain..." |

## Words to NEVER Use

- "Obviously" / "Clearly"
- "Just" (minimizing: "just do this")
- "Actually" (condescending tone)
- "Policy says..." (cold/bureaucratic)
- Competitor names (don't compare)
- Internal jargon (sprint, backlog, etc. unless customer uses it)

## Response Structure

### Standard Response
1. **Greet** — Personalized greeting
2. **Acknowledge** — Show you understand the inquiry  
3. **Solve** — Provide clear steps or answer
4. **Confirm** — Ask if they need more help
5. **Close** — Appropriate sign-off

### Escalation Response  
1. **Greet** — Personalized greeting
2. **Acknowledge** — Show empathy for the situation
3. **Explain** — What's happening next (escalation)
4. **Set Expectations** — Timeline for response
5. **Offer** — Anything else in the meantime
6. **Close** — Reassuring sign-off
